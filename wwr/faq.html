This trip is an absolute delight for children of all ages, and we have observed that those over the age of 8 tend to have an even more enjoyable experience (which parents appreciate too!). While concerns about children getting bored on a boat for extended periods are common among parents, we are thrilled to share that we rarely encounter bored children, regardless of their age. The areas we navigate through are simply breathtaking, showcasing some of the world's most stunning landscapes, and surprises await around every corner. Moreover, the exhilarating rapids add an extra element of excitement to the journey. Rest assured, each boat is accompanied by a knowledgeable guide who not only ensures safety but also entertains with fascinating stories—some of which may even be true!
We understand that safety is a top priority when it comes to adventure sports like whitewater rafting. Rest assured that we take safety seriously and have measures in place to ensure your well-being throughout the experience. Our guides are highly qualified, with certifications in Wilderness First Aid and a minimum of 3 years of rafting experience. It's important to note that most injuries that occur during rafting trips are minor, such as sunburns or land-based accidents. Additionally, all participants are required to wear Coast Guard-approved life vests or personal flotation devices (PFDs), regardless of their swimming abilities. We want everyone to enjoy the thrill of whitewater rafting, including non-swimmers. Our experienced guides will provide a comprehensive safety briefing before the trip, explaining what to expect and how to react in the unlikely event of falling out of the raft. Your safety and enjoyment are our utmost priorities, and we strive to create a safe and memorable experience for all participants.
Our trip package includes all the essential camping equipment you'll need for a comfortable stay. You can expect to receive a cozy sleeping bag, a foam pad for added comfort, a waterproof duffle bag, a small waterproof day bag, a tent, and a comfortable cot. Additionally, we provide eating utensils and a camping chair for your convenience. Throughout the day, we offer refreshing water and lemonade to keep you hydrated. In the morning and evening hours, you can enjoy a variety of hot beverages such as coffee, tea, and hot chocolate. While we provide these beverages, you are also welcome to bring your own beer, liquor, and soft drinks (within reasonable limits of 2 cans per day). You have the option to purchase these items either the night before the trip or on your way to the starting point. To keep you energized and satisfied, we provide three delicious meals per day, along with snacks to keep your hunger at bay. Rest assured that we strive to make your trip enjoyable and worry-free by taking care of these essential provisions.
To effectively prepare for temperature and weather fluctuations in the Canyon, it is important to anticipate both hot and dry as well as cold and wet conditions. Consider the following recommendations for your clothing and gear:
  • Ensure you have a reliable rain jacket and rain pants; avoid relying on ponchos.
  • Pack quick-drying shirts and shorts for convenience and comfort.
  • Opt for long-sleeve cotton shirts and pants to stay cooler on hot days.
  • Button-down shirts provide added sun protection for your neck.
  • Choose lightweight cotton pants, nylon pants, or a sarong for versatility.
  • Men may find swim trunks suitable as shorts, especially if they are prone to feeling cold.
  • If you are sensitive to cold temperatures, bring polypropylene long underwear as a base layer (top and bottom).
  • Women can wear swim suits or quick-dry underwear with sport bras as a base layer during the day.
  • Layer with quick-dry shorts and a suitable shirt (polypropylene if you get cold, cotton if you get hot).
  • Two-piece swim suits are convenient for changing or using the restroom.
  • Protect yourself from the sun with a hat featuring a large brim and a high-quality strap.
